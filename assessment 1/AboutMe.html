About Me

With a background in costume design and production, my love for working with my hands extends to all materials. The decision to major in woodwork/metalwork while minoring in textiles was to give myself the opportunity to learn more about materials I am unfamiliar with. My decision to teach TAS stems from my own experiences at high school, which while not always positive I found refuge in the loving and knowledgeable hands of the TAS department and discovered that finally, there where other people that spoke my language.

I have recently been diagnosed with a range of learning disabilities. I aspire to help young adults who are struggling to come to terms with their differences and show them it's ok to excel at making skills and struggle with the theory components, this diversity does not make you any less intelligent than the math's genius, just different.

AND THAT DIFFERANCE SHOULD BE CELEBRATED.

I plan to teach by modeling, class discussion, student based focus, divergent thinking and by making connections to the modern day world. I hope this approach will service as a welcome breath of fresh air to students that might struggle in theory centered subjects. I hope to help inject life into TAS subjects while shifting the perspectives from a second class subject to necessary life skill.
